# Consumables and Stowage

## Typical on-orbit stockage (for 6-person crew)

| Consumable | Approximate stockage | Duration (6 crew) |
|---|---|---|
| Food | 45–90 days | |
| Drinking water | 200+ L reserve | weeks with WPA; days without |
| Oxygen (stored) | tens of kg | days |
| LiOH canisters | dozens | days (as CDRA backup) |
| PBE O₂ cartridges | multiple per crew | hours each |
| Spare PBE units | ≥ 1 per crew | — |
| Fire extinguishers (PFE) | multiple per module | — |
| Medical supplies | 90+ days | — |
| Crew hygiene | 60+ days | — |

Resupply arrives every 1–3 months via Progress, Dragon cargo,
Cygnus, or HTV-X. The station maintains ~2× nominal consumption
in reserves at all times.

## During an emergency

- **Oxygen** — OGA/Elektron supply continues unless they go offline.
  If both offline (rare), stored O₂ plus SFOG candles buys days.
- **Water** — WPA continues unless contaminated. Stored drink bags
  buy additional days.
- **CO₂ scrubbing** — CDRA + Vozdukh. Backup LiOH. A few days of
  LiOH is available, and is the fallback if CDRAs fail.
- **Food** — not time-critical; weeks of reserves on hand.
- **Medical** — CMO-accessible kit includes resuscitator,
  medications (bronchodilators, O₂ therapy, analgesics), and
  ultrasound for remote-guided diagnosis.

## LiOH canister detail

- Each canister absorbs ~75 L of CO₂, enough for one crewmember
  for ~6 hours.
- 6 canisters/day for full crew.
- Stockage typically ~30–50 canisters.
- Used passively — canister is opened and placed in a ventilated
  location, no power required.
- Exothermic; canister surface warms slightly during absorption.

## Spare parts and ORUs

Hundreds of Orbital Replacement Units stowed throughout the
station. ORUs are designed for crew-swap without specialized
tools:

- Spare CDRA zeolite beds.
- Spare OGA electrolyzer stacks.
- Spare pumps (IATCS, ammonia).
- Spare batteries (EVA-installable on truss).
- Spare avionics modules.

Ground tracks ORU inventory via the IMS (Inventory Management
System) and calls crew to the correct stowage location by
location code.

## Role in ammonia emergency

Immediately after the ammonia event, filter saturation may
require consumption of:

- One or more TCCS charcoal cartridges (scheduled for next
  resupply).
- Potentially LiOH canisters if CDRA(s) are contaminated.
- PBE O₂ bottles consumed during the response (replaceable from
  stockage).

None of these are likely to be the limiting factor within the
timeline of the emergency and immediate recovery.
