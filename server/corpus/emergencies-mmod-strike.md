# Micrometeoroid and Orbital Debris (MMOD) Strike

## Threat characteristics

The ISS is hit by micrometeoroids and human-made orbital debris
continuously at the small scale (dust grains to flecks of paint).
The station's multi-layer shielding (Whipple shields and stuffed
Whipple designs) defeats particles up to roughly 1 cm at typical
impact speeds (~10 km/s for MMOD, ~15 km/s for debris).

Impacts larger than ~1 cm can penetrate the pressure shell,
producing a rapid depressurization scenario.

## Conjunction warnings vs. unannounced impacts

- **Conjunction (expected)** — ground tracks cataloged objects and
  predicts close approaches days in advance. If a close approach
  crosses the defined safety volume, the station performs a
  Debris Avoidance Maneuver (DAM) using propulsion from a docked
  vehicle (Progress/Cygnus) or station thrusters.
- **Unannounced impact** — an object too small to be cataloged, or
  one whose trajectory was mispredicted, hits without warning. This
  is the scenario that produces the rapid-depress alarm.

## Crew response to an unannounced strike

Same as rapid depressurization. The fact that the cause is MMOD
rather than equipment failure does not change the immediate actions
(PBE optional initially if no ATM alarm, muster, isolate, find
leak, patch or retreat).

## Shelter-in-place for predicted conjunctions

For a predicted high-risk conjunction where a DAM is not possible,
the crew shelters in place in their return vehicles during the
pass window. Hatches are closed between the vehicles and the
station. Duration: typically 10–30 minutes from AOS of the
conjunction event to LOS.

If the station is struck during shelter-in-place, the crew is
already in a lifeboat and can undock if the impact produces
catastrophic damage.

## Post-event inspection

After any suspected MMOD event — even one that did not produce a
depress alarm — the crew inspects for evidence of an impact on an
EVA. A near-miss that damages shielding without penetrating may
have reduced future protection and needs ground-led analysis.
