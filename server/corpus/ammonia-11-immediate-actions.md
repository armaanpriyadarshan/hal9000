# Ammonia Leak — Immediate Actions (First 60 Seconds)

The first minute after an ATM alarm is fully memorized, no-procedure-needed
execution. Every crewmember drills this until it is automatic.

## 1. Don Portable Breathing Equipment (PBE) — 0–30 seconds

Each pressurized segment has PBE stowage locations. PBE units are
demand-flow oxygen masks with a 15-minute bottle.

**Correct donning sequence:**

1. Grab the PBE case, open, pull the mask by the harness.
2. Pull the harness over the head, position the mask over nose and mouth.
3. Tighten the lower straps, then the upper straps — lower first so the
   mask seats on the chin.
4. Open the oxygen cylinder valve fully.
5. Seal-check: exhale hard into the mask; check for leak around the edge.
   If the seal is bad, re-tighten straps. Do not remove the mask to retry.
6. Speak: "Mask on, good seal." — all crew confirm to each other.

A correctly donned PBE protects the crew's airway at the ~300 ppm IDLH
level for the full cylinder duration.

## 2. Group the crew in one module — 30–45 seconds

The on-duty crewmember calls "Muster in [node]." Default muster point is
usually Node 1 (Unity), centrally located between US and Russian segments,
with access to Soyuz/Dragon safe-haven vehicles.

If any crewmember cannot reach the muster point (e.g., they are
EVA-prepping in an airlock), the on-duty commander makes the call whether
to wait or proceed with hatch closure.

## 3. Close hatches to isolate affected segment — 45–60 seconds

Once mustered, close the hatches between modules to contain the ammonia
to the source segment. The US and Russian segments can be isolated from
each other by closing Node 1's forward and aft hatches.

Hatch-closure rules:

- Close hatches **away from** the suspected source first.
- If the source is unknown, close all non-muster hatches.
- Verify each hatch is latched (visual indicator shows LATCHED, not just
  closed).
- Do not open a hatch once closed until the module air is confirmed safe.

## After the first minute

With the crew masked, mustered, and isolated, the time pressure drops
from seconds to minutes. The next phase is diagnosis: which segment has
the leak, and can the source be isolated without abandoning that segment?

See `12-safe-haven.md` for muster-point selection and
`16-leak-isolation.md` for diagnosis.
