# Daily Crew Schedule

## Operational timeline

All ISS activities are scheduled against **Greenwich Mean Time
(GMT)**. The crew's workday aligns to GMT, which is typically
offset from the local sunrise/sunset pattern the crew experiences
(16 per day in orbit).

### Nominal weekday

- **06:00 GMT** — Wake, personal hygiene, breakfast.
- **07:30 GMT** — Daily Planning Conference (DPC) with ground.
- **08:00–13:00 GMT** — Morning work block: experiments, maintenance.
- **13:00–14:00 GMT** — Lunch + exercise.
- **14:00–19:00 GMT** — Afternoon work block.
- **19:30 GMT** — Evening Planning Conference (EPC).
- **20:00 GMT** — Dinner + personal time.
- **21:30 GMT** — Sleep.

Crew get ~8.5 hours of scheduled sleep. Actual sleep tends to be
shorter due to microgravity-related insomnia.

### Weekends

Partial work-days, mostly housekeeping and personal time.
Emergencies override the schedule at any time.

## Exercise

Every crewmember has **2.5 hours** of scheduled exercise daily
(aerobic + resistive). Critical for musculoskeletal health; not
negotiable.

## Planning products

- **OOHA (On-Orbit Hours Available)** — daily work time budget.
- **IMS (Inventory Management System)** — tracks every stowed item.
- **OSTPV** — the electronic crew schedule, viewable on station
  laptops.

## During emergencies

Schedule is suspended. Commander has authority to defer any
scheduled activity. Mission Control coordinates post-event
rescheduling.

## During LOS

If an LOS gap means no ground-coordinated schedule handoff, the
crew continues with the last-published schedule until AOS. The
pre-published OSTPV covers at minimum the following 24 hours.
