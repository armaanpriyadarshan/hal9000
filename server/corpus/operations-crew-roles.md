# Crew Roles and Positions

## Commander (CDR)

- Overall authority on station; ultimate decision-maker during
  emergencies.
- Ensures crew safety; calls abandon-ship if necessary.
- Primary point of contact with Mission Control.

## Flight Engineer (FE)

- Support crew; multiple FE positions (FE-1, FE-2, etc.).
- Each FE may have designated specialties (robotics, Russian
  segment systems, science payloads, EVA).
- Execute CDR's orders during nominal and emergency operations.

## Station size

- Nominal crew: 7 (three Soyuz seats + four Dragon/Starliner
  seats). Sometimes reduced to 6 during vehicle turnover.
- Maximum short-term during visiting vehicle handover: up to 11.
- Minimum during contingencies: 3 (one Soyuz's worth), though
  3-crew operation is rare.

## Training baseline

All crewmembers train in:

- Emergency procedures (fire, depress, toxic atm — memorized
  immediate-action drill).
- PBE donning and use.
- Robotic arm operations (at least basic).
- Medical response (CMO designation for the medically trained
  crewmember).
- Their specific scheduled science payload operations.

Some crewmembers train additionally in:

- EVA — spacewalk-qualified, multiple EVAs per increment typical.
- Russian-segment systems — crew on Soyuz train Russian language
  and systems to an operational level.
- Robotics — Canadarm2 / Canadarm2 plus the Japanese arm.

## CMO — Crew Medical Officer

One crewmember per increment is designated Crew Medical Officer.
They are the primary responder for any medical event on station,
trained to intermediate-EMT level plus spacecraft-specific
scenarios. They have access to the station's medical kit
(Advanced Resuscitator, medications, ultrasound for remote-guided
diagnosis).

During an ammonia event, the CMO assesses symptomatic crew
during and after exposure and makes the recommendation for
on-station treatment vs. evacuation.

## Ground interaction

Each crewmember has an assigned **Capcom** (Capsule
Communicator) for voice loops — a fellow astronaut serving as
the voice of Mission Control. Direct scientist-to-crew comm is
routed through flight controllers, not direct.

## HAL interaction

An on-device AI assistant (HAL) supplements crew training during
LOS periods. HAL answers questions from any crewmember, prioritizes
CDR's directives when there's conflict, and defers to the CMO on
medical questions.
