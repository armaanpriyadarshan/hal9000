# Crew Health Monitoring

## Baseline vitals

Nominal ranges for ISS crew (adults, at microgravity-adapted
state, ~3+ months post-launch):

- **Heart rate:** 55–85 bpm at rest. Slight lower than
  pre-flight baseline.
- **Blood pressure:** 100–140 / 60–85 mmHg. Cephalad fluid shift
  raises intracranial-area pressure vs. pre-flight baseline.
- **SpO₂:** ≥ 96% on ambient cabin air.
- **Respiratory rate:** 12–18 breaths/min at rest.
- **Body temperature:** 36.5–37.3 °C (core).

## Microgravity adaptations (normal but different from Earth)

- **Facial puffiness** from fluid shift (caput medusae effect).
- **Muscle atrophy** in antigravity muscles without exercise.
- **Bone loss** ~1% per month without countermeasures.
- **Cardiovascular deconditioning** — hearts shrink ~10%.
- **Vision changes** — some crewmembers develop SANS
  (Spaceflight-Associated Neuro-Ocular Syndrome).
- **Immune changes** — latent virus reactivation common.

These are tracked, not emergencies. Countermeasures: daily
exercise, nutrition, adequate sleep.

## Red flags during an emergency

Any of these warrant immediate CMO attention:

- **Stridor** (wheezing on inhale) — airway compromise.
- **Cyanosis** — blue lips or fingertips — hypoxia.
- **SpO₂ below 90%** on cabin air — significant hypoxia.
- **Loss of consciousness** — any cause.
- **Severe chest pain** — rule out cardiac event, pneumothorax.
- **Severe headache + confusion** — possible hypoxia or CO
  exposure.

## Exercise routines

- **Advanced Resistive Exercise Device (ARED)** — cable-driven
  resistance device in Node 3. Provides weightlifting analog.
- **Treadmill (T2/COLBERT)** — harness-restrained running.
- **CEVIS (Cycle Ergometer with Vibration Isolation System)** —
  stationary bike.
- **Soyuz bungees / VELO** — on Russian segment.

Missing exercise during a multi-day emergency is acceptable;
resume as soon as event resolution allows.

## Radiation

The crew absorbs roughly 0.5 mSv/day at ISS altitude, ~200× the
Earth-surface dose. Cumulative tracking via personal dosimeters
(PRD). Solar particle events can increase the instantaneous rate
100×+; crew retreats to the more-shielded Russian segment during
high-SPE events when alerted by ground.

## Post-ammonia-exposure monitoring

See `ammonia/14-toxicity-health.md`. Key: watch for delayed
pulmonary edema 24–48 h after exposure. Any symptomatic
crewmember should be within line-of-sight of the CMO for that
window.
