# Ammonia Toxicity and Crew Health

## Dose-response for inhaled ammonia

| Concentration (ppm) | Human effect |
|---|---|
| 5 | Detection threshold by smell for most people |
| 25 | Upper limit for 8-hour OSHA workplace exposure |
| 50 | Mild eye and throat irritation within minutes |
| 100 | Moderate irritation; cough reflex; lacrimation |
| 300 | **IDLH** — Immediately Dangerous to Life or Health |
| 500–1000 | Severe respiratory distress within minutes |
| >2500 | Fatal with short exposure |
| >5000 | Rapidly fatal |

Ammonia is water-soluble and deposits primarily in the upper airway
(eyes, nose, throat, upper lungs) rather than the alveoli. Injury is
chemical-burn-like: the ammonia combines with moisture to form ammonium
hydroxide, a strong base.

## Onboard symptoms to watch for

- **Eye symptoms:** stinging, lacrimation, difficulty keeping eyes open,
  visible redness. Severe exposure causes corneal damage.
- **Upper airway:** sore throat, cough, hoarseness. Stridor (wheezing
  on inhale) indicates laryngeal edema — medical emergency.
- **Lower airway:** wheezing, shortness of breath, chest tightness.
  Delayed pulmonary edema can develop 24–48 hours after exposure even
  in crewmembers who initially felt recovered.
- **Skin:** chemical burns on exposed skin, especially eyes and face.
  Liquid ammonia contact causes frostbite from rapid evaporation plus
  chemical burn.

## First aid

**For eyes:** immediate continuous irrigation with water for at least
15 minutes. Use the US Lab or Node 3 eye-wash stations. Do not
neutralize with acid — irrigation only. Remove contact lenses before
irrigation.

**For skin:** flood with water. Remove contaminated clothing.

**For respiratory symptoms:** 100% oxygen by demand mask (not PBE
cylinder — switch to long-duration supplemental O₂). Humidified oxygen
if available. Positive pressure ventilation if the crewmember is
unable to maintain airway.

**For ingestion** (unlikely on-orbit but possible with liquid
contamination of food/water): water, do not induce vomiting, do not
give activated charcoal (ineffective).

## Decontamination of crew

After unmasking:

1. Remove clothing worn during exposure; bag and isolate.
2. Full-body wipe-down with wet towels; pay attention to hair and
   skin folds where ammonia can linger.
3. Change into clean clothing.
4. Monitor for delayed respiratory symptoms for 48 hours.

## Medical evacuation threshold

Return to Earth is warranted for:

- Any crewmember with stridor, persistent severe cough, or SpO₂ below
  92% on ambient cabin air.
- Corneal injury not resolving within 6 hours of irrigation.
- Any crewmember unable to maintain normal activities.

Dragon or Soyuz can return three crew within hours; plan re-entry
window in coordination with ground when comm is available.
