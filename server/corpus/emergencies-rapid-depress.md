# Rapid Depressurization

## Detection

Cabin pressure sensors on each segment detect a pressure drop rate
faster than the station's nominal makeup-gas system can compensate.
A sustained drop rate above ~1 mmHg/min triggers the ΔP alarm.

## Time to act (Time of Useful Consciousness, TUC)

The crew must identify and isolate the leak before cabin pressure
falls to a level that risks rapid hypoxia. TUC depends on the leak
rate:

| Cabin pressure | Approximate TUC |
|---|---|
| 8.0 psi | Hours (habitable with some performance degradation) |
| 7.0 psi | 30+ minutes |
| 5.0 psi | Minutes |
| 4.0 psi | Less than 1 minute without supplemental oxygen |
| 3.5 psi | Loss of consciousness in seconds |

Baseline cabin is 14.7 psi. A rupture large enough to drop from
14.7 to 5 psi in under 20 minutes is the "rapid depress" scenario
the crew drills.

## Immediate response

1. **Alert all crew** — commander calls depress, segment name.
2. **Muster** in a module away from the suspected leak source.
3. **Close hatches** to isolate the leaking module.
4. **Monitor pressure** in each module. The module whose pressure
   continues to drop after hatch closure is the leaking module.

If the leak is in a module the crew cannot abandon (e.g., a module
they must transit through), consider isolating the remainder of
the station and retreating to the lifeboat vehicles.

## Finding the leak

Once the leaking module is identified and isolated, the crew can
search for the breach:

- **Audible hiss** is the most common signature. In the quiet of a
  depressurizing module, the breach is often audible from several
  meters.
- **Ultrasonic leak detector** — handheld tool that picks up the
  ultrasonic component of a leak; more sensitive than ear.
- **Visible moisture or ice** — air escaping rapidly chills the
  surrounding structure; ice crystals can form at the leak edge.

## Patching

Small leaks (pinhole to ~1 cm) can be patched with:

- **Kapton tape** with backing — pressed over the hole, stays put
  by atmospheric pressure once applied.
- **Crew-installed patch kit** — designated flight kits with
  pre-cut patches.

A patched leak can stabilize cabin pressure while ground works on
permanent repair plans. Larger breaches may not be patchable and
require module isolation permanently.

## If patching fails

Lifeboat egress per `ammonia/17-evacuation.md` sequence.
Crew-to-seat assignments the same.
