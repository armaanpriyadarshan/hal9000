# Docking and Berthing Ports

The ISS has many ports where visiting vehicles dock or cargo
modules berth. They are grouped by mechanism.

## Active docking (IDSS/APAS)

- **IDA-2** (International Docking Adapter) — on Harmony forward.
  Crew Dragon and Starliner.
- **IDA-3** — on Harmony zenith. Second commercial crew port.
- **PMA-2/PMA-3** — adapt the IDAs to Harmony's CBM berthing. PMA-1
  joins Node 1 and Zarya.

## Common Berthing Mechanism (CBM)

- Used for module-to-module permanent berthing and for cargo
  vehicles (Cygnus, Dragon cargo) that are captured by the
  robotic arm (Canadarm2) and berthed rather than docking under
  their own control.
- Ports: Node 1, Node 2, Node 3, Destiny, Columbus, Kibo, PMM.

## Russian-segment SSVP ports

- **Zvezda aft** — Soyuz/Progress.
- **Zvezda nadir** — Soyuz/Progress.
- **Zarya nadir (via Rassvet)** — Soyuz/Progress.
- **Zvezda zenith (via Poisk)** — Soyuz/Progress.
- **Nauka nadir (via Prichal)** — multiple Soyuz/Progress ports.

## Typical vehicle assignments

- **Soyuz MS** — Russian segment docking ports. Usually 2 Soyuz
  present.
- **Progress** — Russian cargo. Usually 1–2 present.
- **Crew Dragon** — Harmony forward or zenith.
- **Dragon cargo** — berthed to Harmony nadir.
- **Cygnus** — berthed to Unity nadir or Harmony nadir.
- **HTV-X / HTV** (JAXA, historically) — Harmony nadir.

## Hatch closure considerations

Every docking port has a hatch. Hatch closure between station
and vehicle is independent of internal bulkhead hatches between
modules.

During emergencies, vehicle hatches may be closed to protect the
vehicle's cabin (which can serve as a safe haven). Internal
bulkhead hatches are closed to isolate station modules.

## Lifeboat assignments

Every crewmember on the ISS has an assigned return vehicle (Soyuz
or Dragon). Assignment is published each increment. The crew
member knows which vehicle they go to during an abandon-ship
event without consulting anyone.
