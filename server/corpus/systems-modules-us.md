# US Segment Modules

The "US Segment" includes modules built by NASA and its
international partners (ESA, JAXA, CSA), all running US-style
avionics and interfacing through Node 1.

## Modules

### Unity (Node 1)

- Connecting node between US and Russian segments.
- Six berthing ports (four radial + two axial).
- Interior pass-through volume; less equipment density.
- Pressurized volume: ~55 m³.
- Often the default muster point for emergencies.

### Destiny (US Lab)

- First US laboratory module; activated 2001.
- Primary US-segment research space; many EXPRESS racks.
- Houses CDRA (primary unit) and MCA.
- Pressurized volume: ~106 m³.

### Harmony (Node 2)

- Forward node connecting to Columbus, Kibo, and visiting vehicle
  ports (IDA-2, IDA-3, Node 2 forward).
- Central distribution for US-segment cooling and comms.
- Pressurized volume: ~65 m³.

### Tranquility (Node 3)

- Installed 2010; houses OGA, second CDRA, WPA, UPA, and crew
  exercise equipment (T2 treadmill, ARED resistive exercise).
- Attached to Node 1 on port side.
- Cupola attached to its nadir port.
- Pressurized volume: ~70 m³.

### Cupola

- Seven-windowed observation module, attached to Tranquility.
- Used for robotic arm operations (Canadarm2) and crew Earth
  observation.
- Small pressurized volume; not a good muster point.

### Columbus (ESA)

- European laboratory module, attached to Harmony's port side.
- Research payloads; some long-duration biology/physics.
- Pressurized volume: ~75 m³.

### Kibo (JAXA, JEM-PM and JEM-ELM)

- Japanese Experiment Module; largest single module on ISS.
- PM (Pressurized Module) + ELM (Experiment Logistics Module) +
  JEM-EF (External Facility) on the exterior.
- Pressurized volume (PM): ~150 m³.

### BEAM (Bigelow Expandable Activity Module)

- Inflatable experiment module, 2016. Low occupancy; primarily
  stowage.

### Quest Airlock

- US-segment airlock for EVAs. Equipment Lock (EL) for suit
  storage, Crew Lock (CL) for depressurization and egress.

### Permanent Multipurpose Module (PMM)

- Attached to Unity. Primarily stowage.

## US-segment hatch standard

All US-segment hatches are the Common Berthing Mechanism (CBM)
style or International Docking System Standard (IDSS) style at
vehicle ports. Manual crew-lockable with visual latched indicator.
