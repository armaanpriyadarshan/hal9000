# When to Abandon the Station

If the ammonia leak cannot be stopped and cabin concentrations continue
to rise past the scrubber's removal rate, the crew evacuates to a
docked return vehicle. The decision is the commander's call, informed
by these thresholds.

## Hard triggers for evacuation

- Cabin ammonia concentration above **IDLH (300 ppm)** after ≥ 30
  minutes of scrubbing, still rising.
- Any crewmember showing severe respiratory distress (stridor, cyanosis)
  that cannot be managed with on-board oxygen.
- Loss of scrubbing capability (both CDRAs down, TCCS offline).
- Fire or rapid-depress event occurring on top of the ammonia event.

## Soft triggers (commander decision)

- Concentration stable but holding above 100 ppm with no sign of
  further reduction.
- Isolation actions completed but source unconfirmed.
- Loss of ground communication during the event (cannot consult
  Mission Control).

## Available return vehicles

- **Soyuz MS** — docked at Russian segment nadir ports. 3 crew
  capacity per vehicle. Usually two Soyuz docked at any time.
- **Crew Dragon** — docked at IDA-2 (Harmony forward) or IDA-3
  (Harmony zenith). 4 crew capacity.
- **Starliner** (when present) — Node 2 forward port.

Current crew-to-seat assignment is published each increment; every
crewmember knows their lifeboat.

## Abandon-ship sequence

1. Commander calls "Abandon station. Soyuz/Dragon egress."
2. Crew move in PBE to their assigned vehicle's hatch.
3. Board vehicle in seat assignment; close vehicle internal hatch.
4. Vehicle commander runs vehicle-closeout checklist (valve settings,
   hatch seal check).
5. Undock on commander's call. Ground controllers manage the actual
   de-orbit if reachable; otherwise vehicle commander executes
   pre-briefed deorbit burn.
6. Nominal Soyuz reentry: ~3.5 hours from undock to landing.
7. Nominal Dragon reentry: ~6–24 hours from undock to splashdown,
   depending on landing-site availability.

## What stays on-station

ISS itself remains in its orbit. If the leak is eventually stopped
from the ground (remote commanding via TDRSS), a future crew can
rendezvous and recover the station. If the leak continues to vent,
ISS remains habitable again once the external loop has fully vented
to space and the IATCS side is fully decontaminated — a weeks-to-months
ground-led recovery effort.

## During transit home

Crewmembers who inhaled ammonia may have delayed pulmonary edema.
On-board medical kit includes bronchodilators and supplemental
oxygen. If landing will be delayed, conserve oxygen: one mask per
symptomatic crewmember, others breathe cabin air.
